The "Help to Buy" scheme has been made to assist eligible middle to low earners to purchase a home through a contribution from the Australian Government of up to 40% of the purchase price for new homes and 30% for existing homes. he eligible participants will be able to purchase property for up to $1.3 million in Sydney and regional centres, and up to $800,000 in the rest of NSW, only with the requirement that the home is the principal place of residence. With about 1/3 available to buyers in NSW, there will be about 10,000 places allocated across the country.

Negative gearing occurs when the costs of out-owning an investment (such as interest, maintenance, and rates) exceed the income it generates. This creates a net loss, which investors are able to deduct from their other income (e.g. salary) so that they can pay less tax, acting as a long-term strategy for potential capital growth. This method is not risk free though, as the investor must have enough cash to cover the shortfall, making it risky if interest rates suddenly rise or if the property is vacant. As tax laws are complex and subject to change, if pursuing this method it would be advised to seek independent financial advice.

Social housing is a source of secure and affordable housing for people with very-low to low incomes. It includes public properties that are managed/owned by Homes NSW, while community housing properties are managed or owned by not-for-profit Community Housing Providers (e.g. Evolve Housing). To be eligible for social housing you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be 18+
  • Provide proof of idenity
  • Australian citizen/permanent resident
  • Repay any previous social housing debts
  • Not own property or assets that meet your housing needs
  • Have a household income within the eligibility limit

The First-home buyer Crisis

Income vs. Housing Cost

In March 2002, the cost of an average house in Sydney was 8.3 times that of the average annual full time wage. While this is of course expensive, the rate which it has been raised to as of 2024 makes it look incredibly cheap. In June 2024, the price of that average dwelling in Sydney is now equivalent to 14.4 years earning the average wage in NSW. To put it more simply, residents in Sydney need to pay $615,000 more in today’s dollars than 20years ago

Mortgage Stress and Afforadability

As housing prices increase all throughout Sydney, the affordability rates decrease, therefore increasing mortgage stress amongst buyers. As of early 2026, mortgage stress remains high, with approximately 1.18million to 1.6million mortgage holders (24%-30%) are considered to be at risk of mortgage stress, which is defined as paying more than 30% of their disposable income on housing. While stress levels may have decreased in 2026 from the peak levels that were reached in 2024-2025, they remain a top financial worry, which heavily impacts households with lower incomes. Sydney is one the the most affected cities by this as the entry-level housing requires over 57% of income to service.
